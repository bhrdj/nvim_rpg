TERMINAL MARKS - SOLUTION
=========================

1. Set mark 'a' at current position
   ma

2. Jump to top of terminal buffer
   gg

3. Set mark 't' here (top)
   mt

4. Jump to bottom of terminal buffer
   G

5. Set mark 'b' here (bottom)
   mb

6. Jump back to mark 'a'
   `a  (exact position)
   'a  (beginning of line)

7. Jump to mark 't' (exact position)
   `t

8. Jump to line of mark 'b' (line only)
   'b

9. Yank from current position to mark 'a'
   y`a

Marks are powerful for:
- Bookmarking spots in long terminal output
- Yanking between marked positions
- Quick navigation without searching
